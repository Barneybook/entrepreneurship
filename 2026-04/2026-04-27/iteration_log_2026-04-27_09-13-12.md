=== Entrepreneurship Iteration Log ===
Timestamp: 2026-04-27_09-13-12
Focus Area: Sustainable everyday products
Trend Summary: 
- Many consumers are seeking eco-friendly alternatives for daily use items like reusable bags, bamboo utensils, biodegradable cleaning products.
- There is growing interest in circular economy models where products are designed for reuse, refill, or composting.
- Brands are leveraging transparency about supply chain and carbon footprint to build trust.
- Subscription models for eco-friendly household goods are gaining traction.
- Local production and sourcing reduce transportation emissions and support community resilience.
Generated Ideas:
1. Refill station network for household cleaning products: Customers bring containers to refill stations located in convenience stores or community centers, reducing plastic waste.
2. Subscription box for zero-starter kits: Monthly delivery of curated sustainable everyday items (e.g., bamboo toothbrush, reusable straw, organic cotton produce bags) with educational content on usage and impact.
3. App-based sharing platform for infrequently used eco-tools: Allows neighbors to share items like electric drills, sewing machines, or compost bins, promoting resource efficiency.
Selected Idea: Subscription box for zero-starter kits
Validation:
- Target customer: Urban individuals aged 25-40 interested in sustainability but lacking time to research products; found via Instagram, eco-blogs, and partnerships with zero-waste stores.
- Problem solved: Overwhelm in choosing sustainable products, lack of knowledge on effective usage, and inconvenience of sourcing multiple items.
- Solution: Curated monthly box with 4-5 items, each accompanied by a QR code linking to usage tips and impact metrics; optional add-ons for refill consumables.
- Channels: Social media marketing targeting sustainability hashtags, collaborations with eco-influencers, pop-up booths at farmers' markets.
- Revenue model: Subscription fee NT$599/month, with optional one-time purchase of past boxes; potential for bulk corporate gifts.
- Biggest assumption: Customers will perceive ongoing value and continue subscription beyond initial curiosity; can be tested via a 3-month pilot with feedback surveys.
Next Steps: Source sample suppliers for bamboo utensils and reusable bags; design a minimal website for pre-orders; run a small-scale test offering to 20 friends for feedback.
