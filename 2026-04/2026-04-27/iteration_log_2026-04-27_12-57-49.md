Focus Area: Health and wellness tracking for specific niches
Trend Summary: 
- Wearable technology (like smartwatches, fitness trackers) continues to grow, with increasing focus on health metrics beyond steps and heart rate, such as sleep quality, stress levels, blood oxygen, and even ECG.
- There is a rise in niche health tracking for specific conditions: e.g., menstrual cycle tracking for women, glucose monitoring for diabetics, pollen tracking for allergy sufferers, posture tracking for office workers, and hydration tracking for athletes.
- Mental wellness tracking is gaining traction, with apps for mood journaling, meditation tracking, and stress management.
- Integration with telehealth and personalized health insights via AI is a trend.
- Privacy concerns and data security are important considerations for users.
- Opportunities exist for simple, low-cost solutions that leverage existing smartphones (no need for additional hardware) or integrate with existing platforms like LINE, WhatsApp, or WeChat.
Generated Ideas:
1. LINE-based hydration reminder and tracker for office workers
2. Simple web app for menstrual symptom tracking and fertility prediction using Google Sheets
3. Posture tracking reminder using smartphone camera and pose estimation API for remote workers

5. Meditation and mindfulness tracker Telegram bot with streak rewards for busy professionals
Selected Idea: LINE-based hydration reminder and tracker for office workers
Validation:
- Target customer: Office workers in Taiwan aged 25-45 who work in indoor settings and often forget to drink water
- How they would find this: Through LinkedIn posts, Facebook groups for office workers, or direct outreach to HR departments
- Problem solved: Forgetting to drink water leads to dehydration, fatigue, decreased productivity, and long-term health issues
- Solution: A LINE Official Account or LINE Notify service that sends periodic reminders to drink water and allows users to log their water intake via simple commands (e.g., '喝了 250ml'). The bot tracks daily progress and provides weekly reports.
- Channels: LINE platform itself for delivery; promotion via LinkedIn, Facebook, and partnerships with corporate wellness programs
- Revenue model: Freemium: basic reminders free; premium features like detailed analytics, customizable reminder intervals, and integration with smart water bottles for NT$150/month
- Biggest assumption: Office workers will actively engage with a LINE bot to track their water intake and find value in the reminders and progress tracking
Next Steps: Create a simple LINE Notify prototype using Google Apps Script to test with 10 office workers for one week, collecting feedback on reminder frequency and ease of logging.
